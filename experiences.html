Work


Co-op Term 1 - University of Waterloo, Dept. of Psychology: Face Processing and Social Cognition Lab


For my first co-op term, I reached out to a Psychology professor whom I had built a rapport with after taking her class. I was hired as a summer research assistant to assist the graduate students with their research studies in a cognitive neuroscience lab.

At first, my responsibilities involved going through recorded videos of previous studies and using software to pinpoint the ROIs (regions of interest) on the researcher's face; these areas include the eyes, nose, mouth, face, and background. In an ongoing research study, the graduate student had individuals with autism participate while wearing eye-tracking technology to detect where they were looking. Researchers hypothesized that people with autism tended to make less eye contact when interacting with others. Once I added the different ROIs to the video, the software would be able to detect where the participant fixated and for how many times.

The bulk of my work term consisted of running EEG data through a pipeline on MATLAB. I fixed a lot of bugs and technical errors along the way and amended the pipeline for future use. I also created a manual for future research assistants or any lab member who was interested. After successfully running all the data through the pipeline, I used EEGLAB to manually pick out and remove brain data that had been disrupted by noise, the surrounding environment, etc. Finally, I used SPSS to analyze and interpret the findings before discussing them with my supervisor.

Some other responsibilities I was tasked with was helping graduate students run their studies. In one study, I ran participants through a 1.5 hour study using eye-tracking technology. In another study, I helped set up and apply an EEG cap to participants using electrodes and gel.
